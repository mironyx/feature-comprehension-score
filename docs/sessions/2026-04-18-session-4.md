## Decisions made

- **Name:** followed the renamed LLD (`finalise_rubric`, not `finalise_rubric_v3` — commit
  59a7941 explicitly renamed it). Issue title still referenced `_v3` but the LLD is authoritative.
- **Overloading over replacement:** the LLD's `CREATE OR REPLACE` of a single function
  would have broken the legacy callers tested by the "no breaking change" BDD spec.
  PostgreSQL's function overloading keeps both signatures live simultaneously — same name,
  different argument lists, different call sites.
- **Tested actual schema, not LLD prose:** the LLD §17.1d snippet referenced a non-existent
  `assessments.questions` column, a non-existent `additional_context_suggestions` column,
  and `status = 'ready'` (not in the state machine). Tests followed the real schema and the
  issue's BDD specs rather than the stale prose. Fed back into the LLD in Step 1.5
  (`/lld-sync 243`).
- **Dropped `security definer`:** LLD prescribed it, but the RPC is called by the
  service-role client which already bypasses RLS — the extra privilege escalation was
  redundant and adds a maintenance surface for no behavioural gain.
